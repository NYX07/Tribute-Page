Osagyefo Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Hero of independence

Here's a time line of Dr. Nkrumah's life on earth:

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (Now Ghana)
  • 1930 - Obtained his teacher's certificate from the Prince of Wales' college at Achimota.
  • 1935 - He arrived in the United States by way of Britain to pursue further education at the Lincoln University, earned a bachelors degree by 1939, and enrolled at Lincoln Seminary and the Ivy League University of Pennsylvania the same year.
  • 1945 - He returned to London and enrolled at the London School of Economics as a PhD candidate in anthropology.
  • 1947 - The United Gold Coast Convention (UGCC) political party, at the suggestion of Ako Adjei, appointed Nkrumah to run the party. He left London that same year and returned to the Gold Coast to run the party.
  • 1949 - He announced the formation of his own political party, the Convention People's Party and left the UGCC.
  • 1950 - He was arrested and imprisoned for three years for inciting a strike that quickly led to violence when the then Governor of the Gold Coast, Charles Arden-Clarke, wouldn't agree to Nkrumah's demand for a constituent assembly to write the constitution for the Gold Coast
  • 1951 - Released from prison after just a year when his party won the 1951 legislative election, the first general election to be held under universal franchise in colonial Africa.
  • 1952 - He became the first Prime Minister of the Gold Coast
  • 1956 - He proposed independence for the Gold Coast under a new name, Ghana
  • 1957 - He led Ghana to independence on the 6th of March 1957 and became the country's first Prime Minister. During the event, he proclaimed "Ghana will be free forever". He also told his new country's citizens that "we have a duty to prove to the world that Africans can conduct their own affairs with eficiency and tolerance and through the exercise of democracy. We must set an example to all Africa." He also married his wife, Fathia Ritzk, an Egyptian Coptic bank worker and former teacher on new year's eve.
  • 1958 - His government pass "An act to prohibit organizations uisng or engaging in racial or religious propaganda to the detriment of any other racial or religious community, or securing the election of persons on account of their racial or religious affiliations, or for other purposes in connection therewith."
  • 1964 - He proposed a constitutional amendment which would make the CPP the only legal party, with Nkrumah as President for life of both party and nation.

  • 1972 - Died of Prostate cancer in Romania
  • 2000 - He was voted African Man of the Millennium by listeners to the BBC World Service, being described by the BBC as a "Hero of Independence", and an "International symbol of freedom as the leader of the first black African country to shake off the chains of colonial rule."
